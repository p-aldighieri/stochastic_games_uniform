# Bootstrap breakdown for Route B: Martin-function bootstrap to a global Nash selector

This is a decomposition of the preferred current bootstrap route, not a completed proof. It preserves the exact theorem-side target: one public-history behavioral profile (\sigma), one threshold (T_0), all sufficiently large finite horizons, and every initial state. It does **not** switch to payoff-set existence.    

## Target

For every finite stochastic game (G) and every (\varepsilon>0), prove the existence of (\sigma\in\Sigma(G)) and (T_0\in\mathbb N_{>0}) such that for all (T\ge T_0), all initial states (s_1\in S), all players (i\in N), and all unilateral deviations (\tau_i\in\Sigma_i(G)),
[
\gamma_i^T(G,s_1,\sigma)\ge \gamma_i^T(G,s_1,(\tau_i,\sigma_{-i}))-\varepsilon.
]

Write (h) for a public history, (s(h)) for its terminal state, and (h^+=(h,a,s')) for a one-step extension.

**Dependency spine:**
[
L1 \to L2 \to L3^\star \to L4 \to L5 \to L6 \to L7 \to L8 \to L9 \to \text{Final}.
]

**Critical lemma:** (L3^\star).

---

## L1. Safe continuation correspondence

**Statement.** For each public history (h), let (m(h)\in[0,1]^N) be the Martin-function lower-safety vector in the continuation subgame from (h). Define (W(h)\subseteq[0,1]^N) by
[
w\in W(h)
\iff
\forall \delta>0\ \exists \sigma^h\ \exists T_h(\delta)\ \forall T\ge T_h(\delta):
|\gamma^T(G,h,\sigma^h)-w|_\infty\le \delta
]
and
[
\gamma_i^T(G,h,\sigma^h)\ge m_i(h)-\delta
\quad\forall i.
]
Then (W(h)) is nonempty and compact for every public history (h).

**Dependencies.** Martin-function / subgame maxmin-safe continuation machinery; finiteness of players, states, and actions.

**Technique hint.** Use compactness of mixed-action spaces and continuity of induced finite-horizon average payoffs to extract uniformly safe attainable limit points.

**Difficulty.** Hard.

**[BREAKDOWN_AMEND]** Replace the exact definition of (W(h)) if the Martin-function source supports a sharper continuation object.

---

## L2. Root-biased nonemptiness

**Statement.** Fix a deterministic bias rule (b(h)\in\mathbb R^N) on public histories. Define
[
W^\star(h):=\arg\max_{w\in W(h)} b(h)\cdot w.
]
Then (W^\star(h)) is nonempty and compact for every (h). In particular, the route can recurse through a canonical promise set rather than arbitrary choices.

**Dependencies.** L1.

**Technique hint.** Apply Weierstrass to compact (W(h)). The bias is only a tie-breaker.

**Difficulty.** Moderate.

**[BREAKDOWN_AMEND]** Change the bias rule to lexicographic, rational-grid, or state-priority tie-breaking.

---

## L3*. One-step uncorrelated self-generation / Nash selector

**Statement.** For every public history (h) and every promise (w\in W^\star(h)), there exist

* an uncorrelated mixed action profile (x_h\in\prod_i\Delta(A_i(s(h)))),
* continuation promises (w(h^+)\in W^\star(h^+)) for every realized extension (h^+=(h,a,s')),
* a leakage term (\eta(h)\ge 0),

such that for every player (i),
[
w_i \le u_i(s(h),x_h)+\mathbb E_{x_h,P}[,w_i(h^+)\mid h,]+\eta(h),
]
and for every pure one-step deviation (a_i'\in A_i(s(h))),
[
u_i(s(h),x_h)+\mathbb E_{x_h,P}[,w_i(h^+)\mid h,]
\ge
u_i(s(h),a_i',x_{-i,h})+\mathbb E_{a_i',x_{-i,h},P}[,w_i(h^+)\mid h,]-\eta(h).
]

Equivalently, each promise (w) is locally self-generated by an **uncorrelated** mixed profile with controlled incentive leakage.

**Dependencies.** L1, L2.

**Technique hint.** Define a correspondence of locally incentive-compatible tuples
[
(x,{w(h^+)},\eta)
]
and prove it is nonempty and admits a selector. The real obstacle is enforcing **uncorrelatedness** together with continuation admissibility and local Nash inequalities.

**Difficulty.** Theorem-sized / extreme.

**[BREAKDOWN_AMEND]** Strengthen the conclusion to support-wise deviation inequalities, or weaken it to a conditional selector theorem if a full proof is unavailable.

---

## L4. Full-tree recursive selector

**Statement.** Assuming L3*, there exists a globally defined assignment
[
h\mapsto (x_h,w(h),\eta(h))
]
on the full rooted public-history tree such that (w(h)\in W^\star(h)) and the one-step promise and incentive inequalities from L3* hold at every history.

**Dependencies.** L2, L3*.

**Technique hint.** Recursion / dependent choice on the countable rooted history tree.

**Difficulty.** Moderate once L3* is available.

**[BREAKDOWN_AMEND]** Split into countable-tree recursion and measurability verification if a reviewer wants those separated.

---

## L5. Assembly into one whole-game behavioral profile

**Statement.** The selector (h\mapsto x_h) defines a single behavioral profile (\sigma\in\Sigma(G)) by
[
\sigma_i(h)=x_{i,h}.
]
This is one whole-game profile, not merely a family ({\sigma^s}_{s\in S}), and it is valid simultaneously for every initial state.

**Dependencies.** L4.

**Technique hint.** Define (\sigma) directly on the public-history domain. If any intermediate construction is state-indexed, splice it through the first observed state and identify common continuation histories thereafter.

**Difficulty.** Moderate.

**[BREAKDOWN_AMEND]** Insert an explicit state-root gluing lemma here if the construction keeps producing (\sigma^s)-families.

---

## L6. Local-to-global control of unilateral deviations

**Statement.** For each player (i), each unilateral behavioral deviation (\tau_i), each initial state (s_1), and each horizon (T), the deviation gain against (\sigma_{-i}) is bounded by accumulated one-step leakages plus endpoint terms. More precisely, there exists a promise process (w_i(h_t)) such that
[
\gamma_i^T(G,s_1,(\tau_i,\sigma_{-i}))
--------------------------------------

\gamma_i^T(G,s_1,\sigma)
\le
\frac{B_i(s_1)+\mathbb E\sum_{t=1}^T\eta(h_t)}{T},
]
where (B_i(s_1)) is uniformly bounded over (s_1\in S).

**Dependencies.** L4, L5.

**Technique hint.** Reduce behavioral deviations to one-step action substitutions against fixed continuation promises. Any punishment content should already be encoded in the continuation promises (w(h^+)), so this lemma must either prove separate deviator-identification unnecessary or explicitly strengthen L3* to carry enough off-path continuation data.

**Difficulty.** Hard.

**[BREAKDOWN_AMEND]** If off-path continuation control is too weak, split this into a one-shot deviation lemma plus a separate off-path punishment embedding lemma.

---

## L7. Telescoping promise inequality

**Statement.** The promise process from L6 telescopes:
[
\mathbb E\sum_{t=1}^T
\Bigl(
w_i(h_t)-\mathbb E[w_i(h_{t+1})\mid h_t]
\Bigr)
======

\mathbb E[w_i(h_1)]-\mathbb E[w_i(h_{T+1})].
]
Hence
[
\gamma_i^T(G,s_1,\sigma)
\ge
\gamma_i^T(G,s_1,(\tau_i,\sigma_{-i}))
--------------------------------------

\frac{\mathbb E[w_i(h_1)]-\mathbb E[w_i(h_{T+1})]+\mathbb E\sum_{t=1}^T\eta(h_t)}{T}.
]

**Dependencies.** L6.

**Technique hint.** Sum the local inequalities of L3* along the realized play path. This is the clean telescoping step already identified as stable in the route memo.

**Difficulty.** Moderate.

**[BREAKDOWN_AMEND]** Normalize endpoint terms here if a different promise convention is adopted.

---

## L8. Leakage schedule and threshold extraction

**Statement.** Choose the leakage schedule so that (\eta(h)\ge 0) and
[
\sup_{s_1\in S}\mathbb E_{G,s_1,\sigma}\Bigl[\sum_{t\ge 1}\eta(h_t)\Bigr]\le \varepsilon/4.
]
Since all promise coordinates lie in ([0,1]), endpoint terms are uniformly bounded. Therefore there exists (T_0) such that for all (T\ge T_0),
[
\frac{1+\sup_{s_1}\mathbb E\sum_{t=1}^T\eta(h_t)}{T}\le \varepsilon.
]

**Dependencies.** L7.

**Technique hint.** Use a summable depth-based budget, for example (\eta_t\asymp \varepsilon 2^{-t}), and boundedness of payoffs/promises for endpoint normalization.

**Difficulty.** Easy to moderate.

**[BREAKDOWN_AMEND]** Replace the depth-summable budget by a block budget if later arguments naturally produce block phases.

---

## L9. Uniformity over every initial state

**Statement.** The same (\sigma) from L5 and the same threshold (T_0) from L8 satisfy
[
\gamma_i^T(G,s_1,\sigma)\ge
\gamma_i^T(G,s_1,(\tau_i,\sigma_{-i}))-\varepsilon
]
for all (T\ge T_0), all (s_1\in S), all players (i), and all unilateral deviations (\tau_i\in\Sigma_i(G)).

**Dependencies.** L5, L8.

**Technique hint.** Take the maximum over finitely many initial states once state-uniform constants are available.

**Difficulty.** Easy.

**[BREAKDOWN_AMEND]** If a state-dependent threshold appears, insert the finite-state max argument explicitly.

## Final glue to the theorem

1. L1 and L2 produce a canonical historywise continuation correspondence of minmax-safe promises.
2. L3* upgrades that correspondence to a local uncorrelated self-generation rule with stagewise incentive control.
3. L4 globalizes the selector over the full public-history tree.
4. L5 turns the selector into one behavioral profile (\sigma\in\Sigma(G)).
5. L6 and L7 convert local incentive inequalities into a global deviation bound.
6. L8 converts cumulative leakage into a uniform-in-(T) regret budget.
7. L9 restores the exact theorem-side quantifiers: one fixed profile, all sufficiently large horizons, every initial state.

So, **conditional on L3***, the route yields the target existence statement for uniform (\varepsilon)-equilibria.

---

## Critical lemma

**L3*** is the hardest step. It is exactly the missing uncorrelated selector theorem on the history tree: one must select, at each history, an **uncorrelated** mixed action and compatible continuation promises that remain inside the safe correspondence (W) and simultaneously satisfy local incentive inequalities. The durable route memo flags this as the theorem-sized unresolved bottleneck. Everything after L3* is mostly recursion, assembly, telescoping, and bookkeeping. Everything before L3* is setup.

## Reviewer-facing verdict

This route is structurally coherent **if and only if** the uncorrelated one-step selector exists. The breakdown therefore localizes the proof burden sharply: solve L3*, or show precisely why current Martin-function machinery cannot produce it.
